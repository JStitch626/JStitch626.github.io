About Me

From Educator to Aspiring Software Engineer...

If you are wondering how a former kindergarten teacher became interested in a career in technology, you wouldn't be the first. After all, it seems like a drastic change of course. While I've always been tech-savvy, I realized my passion for tech far outweighed my passion for teaching when COVID drastically changed the world of education.

When COVID-19 spread throughout the United States in March 2020, in-person classes ceased and educators had to adapt to virtual teaching, forcing them to find new and creative ways to connect with students.

I learned the valuable lesson that not only did we have to teach our kindergarten students how to navigate this new online platform, but we had to teach their parents as well, all while learning how to do it ourselves. It was a steep learning curve for everyone.

I began creating easy-to-follow guides for parents, but had no place to house them. The following school year, I left teaching to become the school's IT Technician: at this pivotal moment, I had my first website building experience using help websites - one for teacher use and the other for parent use. You will find the links listed among my other projects.

In the two years I spent as the school's IT Technician, I not only updated my own help websites, but also managed the school's website content using Educational Networks content management system. It was during this time that a friend encouraged me to take the Software Engineering TripleTen bootcamp.

And so begins this new adventure...
